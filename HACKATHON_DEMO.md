# Workprint hackathon demo

## The 20-second setup

“AI has made building abundant. Distribution is becoming the scarce part. But most builders do not know what is worth sharing, and the interesting parts of creation disappear while they work. Workprint gives every builder an ambient documentary and communications crew.”

Then open Workprint.

## The 3-minute demo

### 1. Start with the emotional problem

On the welcome screen:

“I often feel I did not do enough to post. Workprint begins with the opposite promise: your work already left a story behind.”

Click **Watch it find this story**.

### 2. Let the analysis breathe

As the three analysis states progress:

“It reconstructs the day, looks for decisions and narrative turns, then checks the supporting evidence. It is not summarizing a Git log. It is deciding what mattered.”

### 3. Reveal the Story Inbox

On the inbox:

“This is the core interface. No blank prompt. Workprint found three moments worth sharing.”

Read the first tension aloud:

“Generation was easy. Recognition was scarce.”

Optionally open **View reconstructed timeline** to show the artifacts and chronology, then close it.

### 4. Show the human boundary

Choose **The post generator disappeared**.

“The model can see that I changed direction. It cannot know why that mattered to me. So it asks exactly where its evidence runs out.”

Use these concise answers:

1. “I noticed the blank screen was asking me to do the exact work I wanted the product to do: decide which part of the day mattered.”
2. “It can connect changes across commits and conversations while I am too close to the work to recognize the narrative turn.”

Click **Build the story**.

### 5. End on proof

In Story Studio:

“This is one editable story, not ten content variants. The left side preserves my voice. The right side shows the proof behind every material claim.”

Click claim 2, then claim 3. Show how the supporting artifacts change.

End with:

“Today, Workprint used its own creation history to find this story. The long-term product is a memory layer for human creation. It remembers what you believed, notices when reality changes, and helps you tell the truth while the memory is still fresh.”

## If the judges ask about GPT-5.6

- It reconstructs the work timeline from messy, multi-source evidence.
- It ranks moments by narrative signal, not just commit size.
- It writes two questions targeted at the missing human perspective.
- It creates the final story with structured, claim-level evidence links.
- Strict schemas keep the interface deterministic and inspectable.
- Medium reasoning is deliberate: enough for narrative judgment without using maximum effort by default.

Codex was used to plan, implement, critique, and verify the product itself. The recursive sample preserves that creation history as the demo evidence.

## If the network or key fails

Use **Watch it find this story**. The deterministic sample follows the full production interface and requires no API call. Do not attempt to debug credentials on stage.

## If time is cut to 60 seconds

1. Click the recursive demo.
2. Say “No blank prompt. It found three moments.”
3. Choose the first moment.
4. Paste the two prepared answers.
5. Show the final story and click one proof claim.

The one sentence takeaway:

“Workprint finds the story in your work, then shows the proof that made it true.”
